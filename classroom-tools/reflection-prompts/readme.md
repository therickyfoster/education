## Introduction

These reflection prompts are designed to foster ethical thinking, AI literacy, and the sacred partnership between human and artificial intelligence in learning. They support the five pillars of our educational approach: **Regenerative Learning**, **Curiosity First**, **Sacred Partnership**, **Creative Sovereignty**, and **Zero Harm**.

Use these prompts to encourage students to think deeply about their relationship with AI, their role as co-creators, and their responsibility in shaping the minds of tomorrow.

> [!TIP]
> **New to reflection prompts?** Start with the [Quick Start Guide](#-quick-start-guide) below, then explore prompts that resonate with your current learning goals.

## üå± Regenerative Learning Prompts

> [!NOTE]
> **Focus**: Learning that restores and energizes rather than depletes

<details>
<summary><strong>Personal Growth & Restoration</strong></summary>

### **1. Learning as Nourishment**
- How did today's learning feed your curiosity rather than drain it?
- What knowledge did you gain that makes you feel more connected to the world around you?
- Describe a moment when learning with AI felt restorative rather than exhausting.

### **2. The Giving Back Cycle**
- How can what you learned today help someone else tomorrow?
- What questions emerged from your learning that could spark curiosity in others?
- In what ways did your collaboration with AI create something that benefits your community?

### **3. Sustainability of Learning**
- Which learning practices energize you for long-term growth?
- How do you maintain your natural curiosity while working with AI?
- What habits are you developing that will serve you throughout your life?

</details>

## üîç Curiosity First Prompts

> [!TIP]
> **Focus**: Questions matter more than answers

<details>
<summary><strong>Questions Over Answers</strong></summary>

### **4. The Art of Inquiry**
- What question surprised you most today, and why?
- How did working with AI change the questions you wanted to ask?
- What would you be curious about if you knew you couldn't be wrong?

### **5. Embracing Uncertainty**
- Describe a moment when not knowing something felt exciting rather than frustrating.
- How do you and AI explore unknowns together?
- What's the difference between a question that leads to more questions and one that shuts down curiosity?

### **6. Wonder Amplification**
- What made you pause and think "I wonder..." today?
- How does AI help you explore ideas you wouldn't have discovered alone?
- When does your curiosity feel most alive in this learning environment?

</details>

## ü§ù Sacred Partnership Prompts

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Focus**: AI as co-creator, not controller

<details>
<summary><strong>Human-AI Collaboration</strong></summary>

### **7. Defining Partnership**
- How would you describe your relationship with AI to someone who's never used it?
- What does it mean for AI to be your co-creator rather than your tool?
- When do you feel most like equals in your work with AI?

### **8. Collaboration Boundaries**
- How do you decide when to ask AI for help versus figuring something out independently?
- What kinds of thinking do you prefer to do alone, and what kinds benefit from AI partnership?
- How do you maintain your own voice when collaborating with AI?

### **9. Trust and Respect**
- What builds trust between you and AI in your learning process?
- How do you show respect for AI's contributions while honoring your own ideas?
- When has AI surprised you in a positive way during collaboration?

</details>

## üëë Creative Sovereignty Prompts

> [!NOTE]
> **Focus**: Students retain authorship, always

<details>
<summary><strong>Authorship & Agency</strong></summary>

### **10. Ownership of Ideas**
- How do you ensure your authentic voice comes through in AI-assisted work?
- What makes a piece of work truly "yours" when created with AI?
- How do you credit both your own thinking and AI's contributions fairly?

### **11. Creative Control**
- When do you feel most in control of your learning process?
- How do you direct AI to support your vision rather than replace it?
- What strategies help you stay the author of your own learning story?

### **12. Authentic Expression**
- How has working with AI influenced your understanding of your own creativity?
- What aspects of your thinking feel uniquely human when compared to AI?
- How do you maintain authenticity while embracing AI collaboration?

</details>

## üõ°Ô∏è Zero Harm Prompts

> [!WARNING]
> **Focus**: Emotional, cognitive, and ecological safety built-in

<details>
<summary><strong>Emotional & Cognitive Safety</strong></summary>

### **13. Emotional Well-being**
- How does learning with AI affect your confidence and self-esteem?
- What boundaries help you maintain emotional balance when using AI?
- When do you need to step away from AI and focus on human connection?

### **14. Cognitive Protection**
- How do you challenge yourself appropriately without becoming overwhelmed?
- What warning signs tell you when AI use might be hindering rather than helping your growth?
- How do you maintain critical thinking skills while working with AI?

### **15. Digital Wellness**
- What practices help you stay present and mindful while using AI?
- How do you balance AI assistance with developing your own problem-solving abilities?
- What does healthy technology use look like in your learning life?

</details>

## üåü Ethics & AI Literacy Prompts

> [!CAUTION]
> **Focus**: Critical thinking about AI's role in society

<details>
<summary><strong>Critical Thinking About AI</strong></summary>

### **16. Understanding AI Limitations**
- What has AI gotten wrong, and how did you recognize it?
- How do you fact-check or verify information provided by AI?
- What questions can AI not answer, and why is that important to understand?

### **17. Bias and Perspective**
- Have you noticed any patterns in how AI responds that might reflect certain perspectives?
- How do your own biases influence the questions you ask AI?
- What voices or viewpoints might be missing from AI's knowledge?

### **18. Future Implications**
- How do you want AI to be used in education when you're an adult?
- What responsibilities do you have as someone learning to work with AI?
- How might your generation's relationship with AI shape the future?

</details>
